## Example 1: API Monitoring Product

### Scenario: PingForge

**The founder:** Solo technical founder. Backend engineer with 10 years of experience. No marketing experience. Can write clearly (good README writer) but has never written marketing copy. No design skills — plans to use a template. Has a working product with 12 beta users. Wants to launch publicly in one weekend.

**The product:** API uptime and latency monitoring. Checks HTTP endpoints at configurable intervals (down to 10 seconds), alerts via Slack, PagerDuty, and webhooks. Dashboard with latency percentiles and historical uptime. Differentiator: setup takes 30 seconds (paste URL, get monitoring), versus competitors that require agents or config files.

**Assets available:**
- Product screenshots: Yes (dashboard, alert Slack message)
- Demo video: No
- Customer testimonials: One informal quote from a beta user
- Customer logos: No (beta users are small startups, not recognizable)
- Metrics: "Monitoring 200+ endpoints" across beta users
- Comparison data: Knows competitor pricing, setup time

**Competitors mentioned:** Datadog (expensive, complex), Pingdom (outdated UI), Better Uptime (closest competitor, similar positioning)

**Traffic plan:** Will post on Hacker News and Twitter. May run Google Ads later if launch goes well.

---

### Situation Assessment

**Launch stage:** Pre-launch (12 beta users, no public page). → Tier 1. Ship a page this weekend. Don't optimize.

**Marketing skill level:** Never written marketing copy, but writes well technically. → Provide headline formulas. Coach, don't hand off. The "technical writing = marketing copy" reframe applies here.

**Design capability:** No design skills, using a template. → Give section order and content. He plugs it in. Don't recommend custom design.

**Available assets:** Has screenshots and one testimonial. No logos, no video. → Use screenshots in hero, testimonial as social proof, "Monitoring 200+ endpoints" as metric. Skip logo bar.

**Time available:** One weekend. → Tier 1 execution. 4–5 sections maximum. Hero + features + one proof point + CTA.

---

### Recommended Tier: Tier 1 — Launch-ready (weekend)

Given the constraints, here's the page plan:

**Section 1: Hero**
- Headline (Formula 2 — Outcome): "Know when your API breaks before your customers do."
- Subheadline (Pattern C — Address objection): "Uptime and latency monitoring with real-time Slack alerts. Paste your endpoint URL and get monitoring in 30 seconds. No agents. No config files."
- CTA: "Start monitoring free"
- Supporting text: "No credit card required. Free for up to 5 endpoints."
- Visual: Screenshot of the Slack alert notification (this is the "aha moment" — the thing the customer actually cares about)

**Section 2: Feature cards (3 cards)**
- Card 1: "30-second setup" — "Paste your endpoint URL. Pick your check interval. Done. No agents to install, no YAML to write."
- Card 2: "Real-time alerts" — "Get notified in Slack, PagerDuty, or any webhook within 30 seconds of a failure. Automatic escalation if unacknowledged."
- Card 3: "Latency tracking" — "See p50, p95, and p99 latency over time. Catch degradation trends before they become outages."

**Section 3: Social proof (lightweight)**
- The one beta testimonial, with name and title
- "Monitoring 200+ endpoints for developer teams"
- Skip the logo bar (not enough recognizable names)

**Section 4: Final CTA**
- "Start monitoring your APIs in 30 seconds."
- CTA button: "Start monitoring free"
- Supporting text: "Free for up to 5 endpoints. No credit card required."

**What's intentionally omitted and why:**
- No pricing section (free tier is mentioned in CTA — a full pricing page can wait until post-launch)
- No comparison table (don't have enough competitive data to be credible; can add after launch)
- No FAQ (the 4-section page answers the core questions; FAQ is Tier 2)
- No "how it works" (the product is simple enough that "paste URL, get monitoring" in the subheadline covers it)
- No demo video (doesn't exist, don't recommend recording one this weekend — screenshots are sufficient)

**Design direction:** Pick a clean, dark-themed template (monitoring tools feel natural in dark mode). Use the PingForge brand color for CTA buttons and key metrics. One accent color. Don't customize beyond that this weekend.

**Post-launch upgrade path:** After launch, if there's traction, the next additions in priority order:
1. "How it works" section with a 3-step visual (sign up → add endpoint → get alerts)
2. Comparison table vs. Datadog and Better Uptime (when he has real comparison data)
3. Pricing page (when there's a paid tier to sell)
4. FAQ section (based on actual questions from launch day)

## Example 2: SEO Keyword Research Tool

### Scenario: KeyScope

**The founder:** Two-person team — one technical founder (backend/data engineer) and one growth/marketing co-founder. The growth co-founder has written marketing copy before, has done SEO, and has reasonable design sense. They use Webflow. The product has been live for 4 months with 340 paying customers. Current landing page exists but converts at 1.8% from Google Ads traffic — below the "okay" benchmark for this category.

**The product:** Keyword research tool focused on keyword gap analysis and opportunity scoring. Users paste their domain and a competitor's domain, and KeyScope shows: keywords the competitor ranks for that the user doesn't, keywords where the user ranks but below the competitor, and an "opportunity score" that factors in difficulty, search volume, and the user's domain authority. Differentiator: opportunity scoring is more accurate than traditional keyword difficulty metrics (they have data to prove this), and the UX is dramatically simpler than enterprise tools.

**Assets available:**
- Product screenshots: Yes (gap analysis view, opportunity score dashboard)
- Demo video: 2-minute product walkthrough recorded by the growth co-founder
- Customer testimonials: 8 strong testimonials, several from known names in SEO community
- Customer logos: ~15, including 3 recognizable SaaS companies
- Metrics: "340+ paying customers", "Tracking 2M+ keywords", "87% of users find their first ranking opportunity within 5 minutes"
- Case study: One written case study with a named customer

**Competitors mentioned:** Ahrefs (industry standard, expensive, overwhelming UI), Semrush (similar, enterprise-focused), SE Ranking (mid-market, decent but generic)

**Traffic sources:** Google Ads (primary, 60% of traffic), organic search (30%), referral/word of mouth (10%).

**Current page problems (founder's assessment):** "The hero doesn't say anything specific. The page feels generic — could be any SEO tool. We have all these testimonials but they're buried at the bottom. The comparison section looks defensive."

---

### Situation Assessment

**Launch stage:** Post-launch, 340 customers. Solid traction but conversion is below benchmark (1.8% from paid search vs. 2–4% "okay" range). → Tier 2 with targeted fixes. This isn't a rebuild — it's a strategic revision.

**Marketing skill level:** Growth co-founder has experience. → Skip copy basics. Focus on messaging strategy, headline testing, and competitive positioning. Coach on specifics, not frameworks.

**Design capability:** Uses Webflow, has design sense. → Give strategic direction, not template recommendations. They can execute.

**Available assets:** Rich. Testimonials, logos, case study, video, strong metrics. → The assets aren't the problem. Asset placement and messaging are the problem.

**Time available:** Ongoing project, willing to invest 1–2 weeks. → Tier 2 with a clear testing plan for Tier 3.

---

### Recommended Tier: Tier 2 — Complete page revision (1–2 weeks)

The page exists and has traffic. The problem is conversion, not existence. Approach this as a strategic revision, not a blank-page rewrite.

**Diagnosis:** Based on the founder's description, the primary issues are:

1. **Hero clarity/specificity** — "Doesn't say anything specific" suggests the headline is generic ("Better keyword research" or similar). The visitor can't tell why KeyScope is different from the 4 other tools they've evaluated today.
2. **Social proof placement** — Strong testimonials buried at the bottom means the highest-trust-building content comes after most visitors have already bounced.
3. **Comparison positioning** — "Looks defensive" suggests the comparison section is framed as "we're not as bad as you think" rather than "here's where we genuinely win."

**Revised page plan:**

**Section 1: Hero (complete rewrite)**
- Headline (Formula 4 — Specificity): "Find the keywords your competitors rank for — and the ones they're missing."
- Subheadline (Pattern B — Specify audience): "Keyword gap analysis with opportunity scoring that actually predicts which keywords you can win. Built for SEO teams and content marketers who are tired of vanity metrics."
- CTA: "Try free for 14 days"
- Supporting text: "No credit card required. See your first opportunities in under 5 minutes."
- Visual: Screenshot of the keyword gap analysis view — the product's core "aha" screen. Annotate it lightly to draw the eye to the opportunity score column.

Why this works better: The headline mirrors the search intent of someone looking for keyword research tools. "Keywords your competitors rank for" is the specific promise. The subheadline differentiates with "opportunity scoring that actually predicts" — a direct contrast to competitor keyword difficulty scores.

**Section 2: Logo bar**
- Show the 3 recognizable SaaS company logos + 5–7 best remaining logos
- Header: "Trusted by 340+ SEO teams and content marketers"

Move this to position 2 (directly after hero). Logos build instant credibility and signal "this is a real product" before the visitor has to read anything else.

**Section 3: Problem validation**
- Framework: Day-in-the-life
- "You export keyword data from three different tools, cross-reference competitor rankings in a spreadsheet, and guess at keyword difficulty. Two hours later, you have a list you're not confident in. There's a better way to find keywords you can actually rank for."

**Section 4: Product demo / walkthrough**
- Embed the 2-minute video, or break it into a 3-step visual walkthrough:
  1. "Paste your domain and a competitor's" (screenshot of input)
  2. "See every keyword gap, scored by opportunity" (screenshot of gap analysis)
  3. "Prioritize the keywords you can actually win" (screenshot of opportunity score list)
- CTA repeat after this section

**Section 5: Feature cards (3 cards, outcome-framed)**
- Card 1: "Keyword gap analysis" — "See exactly which keywords your competitors rank for that you don't. No manual spreadsheet work. Updated weekly."
- Card 2: "Opportunity scoring" — "Our scoring model factors in your domain authority, keyword difficulty, search volume, and SERP features to predict which keywords you can realistically win. 2x more accurate than traditional KD scores." (If they have the data to support this.)
- Card 3: "5-minute time to value" — "Paste two URLs. Get a prioritized keyword list. 87% of users find their first ranking opportunity in under 5 minutes."

**Section 6: Social proof (testimonials)**
- 3 strongest testimonials with photos, names, titles
- Prioritize testimonials that include specific outcomes: "Found 340 ranking opportunities our agency had missed entirely."
- Link to the full case study from one testimonial

**Section 7: Comparison section (reframed)**
- Don't frame as defensive. Frame as "who this is for vs. who it's not for."
- Table comparing KeyScope vs. enterprise tools (Ahrefs, Semrush):

| | KeyScope | Enterprise tools |
|---|---|---|
| Time to first insight | 5 minutes | 30+ minutes |
| Pricing | $49–149/mo | $99–449/mo |
| Keyword gap analysis | Core focus | One of 50 features |
| Opportunity scoring | Predictive model | Basic keyword difficulty |
| Learning curve | Minimal | Significant |

- Positioning: "If you need a full enterprise SEO suite with rank tracking, site audits, and backlink analysis, Ahrefs or Semrush is the right choice. If you need to find your best keyword opportunities fast and act on them, that's what we built."

This reframe is confident, not defensive. It concedes the competitor's strengths (broader feature set) while claiming the specific territory KeyScope wins on (speed, focus, accuracy of opportunity scoring).

**Section 8: Pricing**
- Show plans with a clear free trial CTA on each
- Emphasize the free trial — this audience wants to try before buying

**Section 9: FAQ**
- How fresh is the keyword data? (Address the data freshness concern directly.)
- Where does the data come from?
- How is opportunity scoring different from keyword difficulty?
- Can I export reports?
- What happens when my trial ends?
- Do you integrate with Google Search Console?

**Section 10: Final CTA**
- "Find your next ranking opportunity in 5 minutes."
- CTA: "Start free trial"
- Supporting text: "14 days free. No credit card required."

**What changed from the current page and why:**
1. Hero rewritten for specificity and search intent match (addresses the "too generic" problem)
2. Logo bar moved from bottom to position 2 (addresses the "social proof buried" problem)
3. Comparison section reframed from defensive to confident positioning (addresses the "looks defensive" problem)
4. Testimonials moved higher and given more prominence
5. Problem section added to create emotional resonance before product sections
6. Each section tied to a measurable claim where possible

**Testing plan (Tier 3, after revision is live):**
- Week 1–2: Measure new baseline conversion rate with revised page
- Week 3–4: A/B test headline — "Find the keywords your competitors rank for" vs. "Stop guessing which keywords to target" (Outcome vs. Pain-relief formula)
- Week 5–6: A/B test social proof placement — logos after hero vs. testimonial after hero
- Ongoing: Monthly CRO audit, test one element per 2-week cycle

Minimum traffic for testing: With ~500+ unique visitors/week from Google Ads, tests should reach significance in 2–4 weeks per test. If traffic is lower, focus on qualitative feedback (session recordings, user interviews) instead of A/B tests.
